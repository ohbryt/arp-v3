#Novel Natural Mitophagy Activators Beyond Urolithin A  
*Emerging compounds, safety profiles, and therapeutic potential*  

---

## 1. Overview  

Mitophagy – the selective removal of damaged mitochondria – is a cornerstone of cellular quality control. While urolithin A (a gut‑microbiome‑derived ellagitannin metabolite) has received considerable attention, a growing body of work identifies additional **natural** molecules that can stimulate mitophagy through diverse pathways (AMPK‑ULK1, SIRT1‑FOXO3, PINK1/Parkin, Nrf2, ROS‑mediated signaling, etc.). The following sections summarize the most promising emerging agents, their reported safety, and therapeutic avenues that are under investigation.  

> **Claim status key** – **Verified**: claim directly supported by the cited source; **Inferred**: logical deduction from source data (e.g., safety inferred from toxicity studies); **Unverified**: no source provided (none used here).
