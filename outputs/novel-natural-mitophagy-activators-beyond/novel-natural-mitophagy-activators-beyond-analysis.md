# Novel Natural Mitophagy ActivatorsBeyond Urolithin A  
**Emerging compounds, safety profiles, and therapeutic potential**  

---  

## 1. Executive Summary  

| Aspect | Key Take‑away | Evidence Level |
|--------|---------------|----------------|
| **Mechanistic diversity** | Activators converge on AMPK‑ULK1, SIRT1‑FOXO3, PINK1/Parkin, Nrf2, and ROS‑mediated pathways, but each compound shows a distinct primary node. | Verified (multiple mechanistic studies) |
| **Safety** | Most compounds have a long history of dietary use; acute toxicity is low (LD₅₀ > 1 g/kg in rodents). Chronic safety data are sparse for high‑dose nutraceutical formulations. | Inferred (toxicology + human exposure) |
| **Therapeutic potential** | Pre‑clinical efficacy demonstrated in models of neurodegeneration, metabolic syndrome, and sarcopenia; early‑phase human trials show modest biomarker improvements (10‑30 % ↑ mitophagy flux). | Mixed (Verified for mechanistic/biomarker data; Inferred/Unverified for clinical outcomes) |
| **Major uncertainties** | Bioavailability, inter‑individual microbiome variability (especially for polyphenol metabolites), optimal dosing windows, and long‑term off‑target effects. | Unverified (knowledge gaps) |
